2. Technical Invention Disclosure

2.1 Problem Statement

Existing Linux cpuidle governors predict idle duration from timer and wake-history signals, while KVM halt-poll and PLE-style heuristics target guest halt or spin behavior. None of those mechanisms consumes the host-visible TDX exit taxonomy that occurs immediately before a CPU may drop into the idle task after short host handling. As a result, a CPU that just handled a burst of fast TDX exits may still select an unnecessarily deep C-state, increasing return-to-vCPU latency and causing avoidable power-performance oscillation.
