2. Technical Invention Disclosure

2.1 Problem Statement

Modern XDP deployments increasingly host flow classification, telemetry tagging, congestion policing, and flow-based IDS or machine-learning-assisted detection logic that often inspect only cache-hot headers yet still execute in the receive critical path. NIC drivers and page_pool consumers would benefit from knowing whether the effective XDP program only reads the first cache lines, dirties only a bounded prefix, or may touch deeper or non-linear data, because RX reuse decisions interact with page_pool recycling, DMA sync-for-device behavior, cache coherency assumptions, and queue-local latency. Today the driver has no verifier-sound, runtime-safe contract for that question. Existing code therefore either assumes the worst and performs full recycle or full sync behavior for every packet, or relies on ad hoc driver-local assumptions that become unsound when programs are replaced, when multi-program dispatchers are used, when `xdp_buff_has_frags()` is true, when helpers mediate packet access, when metadata is touched, or when `bpf_xdp_adjust_head()` or `bpf_xdp_adjust_tail()` shifts the packet base. Any optimization that ignores those cases risks stale hints, use-after-free on program replacement, or incorrect buffer reuse on architectures or DMA paths that require stricter synchronization.

2.2 Prior-Art Gap

Existing verifier facts are acceptance-oriented safety bounds, not a stable in-kernel driver contract. A single max-offset or max-access summary cannot distinguish read-only parsing from packet writes, cannot represent sparse footprints such as reads in bytes 0-63 and writes in bytes 192-223 without pessimistically collapsing the entire span, cannot isolate metadata accesses from packet-body accesses, and cannot encode the invalidation effect of successful head or tail adjustment, unresolved tail calls, or helpers whose packet effect is not explicitly declared. That lack of structure matters in two concrete RX fast-path decisions that prior art cannot safely enable: first, whether a driver may preserve a direct recycle path when the XDP program is proven to be prefix-read-only, and second, whether the driver or page_pool may reduce `dma_sync_single_range_for_device()` or `page_pool_dma_sync_for_device()` to only the first 64, 128, 192, or 256 bytes rather than synchronizing the entire receive buffer. Prior mechanisms also do not define a defensive internal interface with version and size fields, do not specify a coordinate system for the summary relative to the entry packet pointer, do not provide an internal helper-effect table for helper-mediated packet reads and writes, do not define conservative rules for multi-buffer XDP frags or unresolved `PROG_ARRAY` contents, and do not publish a single RCU-safe per-RX-queue bundle that drivers can consume without peeking into verifier internals. The fixed 256-byte prefix and 64-byte quanta are therefore not a restatement of existing facts; they are the minimum geometry that preserves driver-actionable distinctions while bounding verifier state and merge cost.

2.3 Proposed Invention

Introduce a verifier-owned internal object `struct xdp_touch_contract_v1 { u16 version; u16 size; u16 prefix_len; u16 quantum_len; u8 rd_mask; u8 wr_mask; u16 flags; u8 policy; u8 reserved[5]; };` stored immutably in `bpf_prog_aux`, and an attach-owned publication object `struct xdp_rxq_touch_bundle { struct rcu_head rcu; u32 generation; struct bpf_prog *prog_root; struct xdp_touch_contract_v1 eff; };` published per RX queue with `rcu_assign_pointer()`. In version 1, `prefix_len` is fixed at 256 and `quantum_len` at 64, so `rd_mask` and `wr_mask` each describe four cache-line-aligned quanta. The contract coordinate system is the entry-time packet data pointer `data0 = xdp_buff->data` at XDP program entry; accesses to `PTR_TO_PACKET_META` set `XDP_TOUCH_F_META_ACCESSED` and are tracked separately from packet masks, and any reachable successful `bpf_xdp_adjust_head()` or `bpf_xdp_adjust_tail()` sets `XDP_TOUCH_F_ADJUST_HEAD` or `XDP_TOUCH_F_ADJUST_TAIL` and forces policy `XDP_TOUCH_POLICY_FULL`. The verifier extracts the contract by classifying all direct packet loads and stores and all helper or kfunc invocations through an internal packet-effect table with categories `NO_PACKET_EFFECT`, `READ_PREFIX`, `WRITE_PREFIX`, `BEYOND_PREFIX`, and `UNKNOWN_PACKET_EFFECT`. Constant or bounded ranges wholly inside bytes 0-255 set the intersecting read or write mask bits; helper calls such as bounded `bpf_xdp_load_bytes`-style reads mark `rd_mask`, bounded store or checksum-rewrite helpers mark `wr_mask`, any access overlapping byte 256 or later sets `XDP_TOUCH_F_BEYOND_PREFIX_RD` or `XDP_TOUCH_F_BEYOND_PREFIX_WR`, and any helper without a declared packet effect or with non-provable bounds sets `XDP_TOUCH_F_HELPER_UNKNOWN` and forces policy `FULL`. The merge lattice is monotonic: masks and flags are OR-combined across branches and loop fixed points, and any state that is beyond-prefix, helper-unknown, adjusting, metadata-ambiguous, or tail-call-unknown escalates to the conservative `FULL` policy. Tail calls are handled conservatively in the base invention: any reachable `bpf_tail_call()` to an unresolved `PROG_ARRAY` sets `XDP_TOUCH_F_TAILCALL_UNKNOWN` and forces `FULL`; an optional narrower embodiment allows attach-time composition across a sealed attach-owned tail-call set whose members are scanned and recomposed under the same attach serialization whenever the set changes. At attach or replace time, under the existing XDP attach serialization such as RTNL and driver XDP setup locks, the kernel computes the worst-case effective contract across the actual dispatcher or multiprogram chain and any sealed tail-call set, fills a fresh `xdp_rxq_touch_bundle`, and RCU-swaps it for each RX queue. Readers are limited to the NAPI or RX poll path: they call `rcu_dereference()` once per poll cycle, may cache the returned bundle pointer only in a local variable inside that RCU critical section, and must never cache it across polls or outside RCU. Drivers and page_pool consumers may consult the effective bundle only in receive recycle and sync logic: if runtime `xdp_buff_has_frags()` is true, or if any invalidating flag is set, they must ignore the masks and use the existing full-sync or slow-recycle path; if `wr_mask` is zero and no invalidating flag is set, a coherent x86 driver may preserve direct recycle without expanding sync coverage; if `wr_mask` is non-zero but bounded, the driver may limit range-based sync to `64 * (highest_written_quantum_index + 1)` bytes when the DMA API and architecture support range sync, otherwise it falls back unchanged. Unknown `version`, `size`, or future `policy` values map to `XDP_TOUCH_POLICY_FULL`, keeping the interface internal, extensible, and safe for in-tree evolution without creating any UAPI or out-of-tree ABI promise.

4. Risks & Mitigations

  • Risk: helper coverage may initially be incomplete, causing false optimism if packet-touch effects are guessed. Mitigation: require an explicit internal helper-effect-table entry for any helper or kfunc that can touch packet memory, and classify every undeclared or non-provable helper as `UNKNOWN_PACKET_EFFECT` that forces `FULL`.
  • Risk: tail-call composition is infeasible for arbitrary mutable `PROG_ARRAY` contents. Mitigation: make the baseline rule any reachable tail call equals `TAILCALL_UNKNOWN` and `FULL`, and keep attach-time composition across tail calls only as an optional sealed-linkage embodiment with serialized membership updates.
  • Risk: multi-buffer XDP and frags can move relevant data outside the linear prefix even when the verifier summary appears small. Mitigation: gate consumption on runtime `xdp_buff_has_frags()` and make frags-present packets unconditionally take the existing full fallback path.
  • Risk: driver authors may cache a contract pointer per RXQ outside RCU and create stale-pointer or use-after-free hazards. Mitigation: publish only a single RCU-managed bundle, require consumption inside the NAPI or RX poll RCU critical section, document cross-poll caching as unsupported, and let non-compliant drivers ignore the hint entirely.
  • Risk: metadata touches, packet-base adjustments, or non-coherent DMA semantics may make a bounded prefix summary insufficient for safe reuse. Mitigation: treat metadata access, `adjust_head`, `adjust_tail`, unknown versions, unsupported architectures, and missing range-sync support as mandatory fallback conditions.
  • Risk: the fixed 256-byte and 64-byte geometry may be challenged as arbitrary. Mitigation: tie those constants to four cache lines of common protocol-header coverage, two-byte mask state, bounded verifier merge complexity, and concrete driver decision points that disappear with 128-byte or 256-byte quanta and do not improve with sub-cache-line granularity.
