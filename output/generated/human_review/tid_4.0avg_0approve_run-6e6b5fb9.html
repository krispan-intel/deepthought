2. Technical Invention Disclosure

2.1 Problem Statement

On Intel TDX hosts, a KVM vCPU task repeatedly crosses the guest-host boundary, but the cost of the next guest reentry is not uniform. A fast interrupt bounce back into the guest, a short host assist, and a heavier private-memory/MMU/coherency service leave different near-term locality footprints and different sensitivity to cross-LLC migration. Linux FAIR/EEVDF sees generic runnable state, util_est/util_avg, wake affinity, cache hotness, capacity fit, asymmetry, and NUMA effects, but it does not see the TDX-private boundary pattern that predicts whether an immediate cross-LLC move is likely to amplify the next reentry cost. The result is avoidable cross-LLC wake migrations of TDX vCPU tasks that increase LLC disruption and p99 latency without materially improving fairness or throughput.
