2. Technical Invention Disclosure

2.1 Problem Statement

In virtio-net guests serviced by vhost-net, packet payload pages often recur inside a small set of guest-physical windows, but vring descriptors, avail and used rings, packed-ring event structures, and transient indirect descriptor tables occupy guest-controlled pages that can sit near or inside those same 2 MiB regions. Generic host hugepage policy cannot distinguish queue-control pages from payload pages, while naive TDP/EPT promotion over a 4 KiB subtree can race memslot generation churn, mmu_notifier invalidations, THP split or migration, dirty-log write-protect, access tracking, readonly flips, nested-virt mode changes, and confidential-memory restrictions. The kernel problem is therefore not just finding hot regions; it is proving, under KVM synchronization, that a 512-GFN aligned window is still metadata-free, still backed by one stable host huge folio, still permission-compatible, and safe to expose with a rate-bounded INVEPT strategy before any concurrent zap, split, or write-protect path invalidates that proof.

2.3 Proposed Invention

The invention introduces an opt-in registration and actuation path centered on a per-VM KVM Virtqueue Arena Registrar. Userspace binds a vhost queue to a VM through new VM ioctls KVM_VHOST_VRING_REGISTER, KVM_VHOST_VRING_UPDATE, and KVM_VHOST_VRING_UNREGISTER, each carrying a vhost fd, queue id, current memslot generation, queue generation, descriptor GPA range, avail GPA range, used GPA range, packed-ring event GPA range if present, and optional extra control-page ranges. KVM validates that these GPAs resolve only inside shared, non-readonly memslots and stores them in a kvm_vqreg object. vhost then gains an internal cookie allowing a bounded helper, kvm_vqreg_report_complete(), to report post-completion payload GPA spans plus any indirect descriptor table page GPAs actually dereferenced while walking the chain. KVM treats registered vring pages as permanently ineligible for promotion and treats observed indirect-table pages as quarantined metadata until explicit queue update or unregister, or until a long no-reuse cooldown under the same queue generation. A background KVM worker bins payload hits by 2 MiB-aligned base_gfn and only attempts promotion when the window is not intersected by static or quarantined metadata pages and when policy gates allow promotion. The promotion path is KVM-only: under memslots SRCU plus the write side of kvm->mmu_lock, KVM proves that the entire 512-GFN window still belongs to one memslot generation, no mmu_notifier invalidation has intervened, all SPTE permissions are merge-compatible, dirty logging and access tracking are off, the memslot is shared and writable, and the host backing resolves to one stable compound folio or huge page spanning the full 2 MiB window. Only after that proof does KVM replace the 4 KiB subtree with a 2 MiB EPT leaf and queue a batched single-context INVEPT or remote TLB flush for visibility. Any later metadata conflict, notifier invalidation, dirty-log write-protect, access-track request, readonly flip, THP split, migration, unmap, or queue lifecycle change causes immediate demotion to 4 KiB or zap. An optional dependent embodiment permits debug-only per-vCPU telemetry roots, but leaf duplication is restricted to registered control pages only, capped at eight 4 KiB leaves per queue and 256 duplicated leaves per VM, excluded from global aging and idle heuristics, and not used for production actuation.

Exact exclusion semantics:
- Any GFN covered by static_meta_ranges is never inserted into a promotion candidate and never merged into a 2 MiB leaf by this feature.
- Any indirect descriptor table page observed by vhost is inserted into indirect_meta_pages_xarray and treated as metadata for promotion purposes until queue update, unregister, memslot generation change, or long cooldown expiry under the same queue generation.
- If a promoted 2 MiB window later intersects a newly observed metadata page, KVM schedules immediate split or zap and starts a cooldown for that window.
- In the optional telemetry embodiment, duplicated control-page leaves are tagged EXCLUDED_FROM_AGING; KVM age walkers, idle-page accounting, reclaim hints, and promotion heuristics must ignore their A or D state so the duplicate leaves do not perturb global memory-aging policy.

Overhead bounds:
- Production promotion mode allocates no alternate EPT roots.
- Debug telemetry mode allocates at most one extra root per vCPU, duplicates only 4 KiB leaves covering registered control pages, caps duplication at 8 leaves per queue and 256 leaves per VM, and therefore bounds extra page-table memory to roughly one root page plus at most 256 extra leaf page-table pages per vCPU in the worst case.
- VM-entry cost in telemetry mode is one root selection at vCPU entry only; there is no in-guest EPTP switching and no VMFUNC dependency.

2.5 Implementation Plan

1. Registration ABI and lifecycle. Add KVM_VHOST_VRING_REGISTER, KVM_VHOST_VRING_UPDATE, and KVM_VHOST_VRING_UNREGISTER on the VM fd. REGISTER is legal only after the VMM has programmed VHOST_SET_OWNER, VHOST_SET_MEM_TABLE, and VHOST_SET_VRING_ADDR for that queue. The payload carries queue_id, current memslot generation, queue generation, vring and control GPA ranges, and a feature bitmap. KVM stores a kvm_vqreg keyed by queue_id. UPDATE atomically replaces metadata ranges, bumps queue_gen, clears prior indirect-meta quarantine for that queue, and marks existing candidates for revalidation. UNREGISTER marks the queue stale, tears down telemetry state, and forces demotion of any promoted window whose queue_gen_mask includes that queue. 2. vhost completion reporting. In the vhost completion path, after descriptor processing is complete, vhost calls kvm_vqreg_report_complete(vqreg, payload_spans, indirect_meta_pages, complete_seq). The helper is strictly bounded: maximum 64 pending records per queue, fixed-size metadata page vector, overwrite-oldest on overflow, and no MMU work on the vhost thread. This makes telemetry lossy but safe. For packed or split rings, vhost reports only actual payload spans and actual indirect-page GPAs touched while decoding the descriptor chain. 3. Candidate formation. A KVM worker drains each completion ring, translates payload spans into 2 MiB-aligned base_gfn buckets, increments hit_count and byte_count, and rejects buckets that intersect static_meta_ranges, indirect_meta_pages_xarray, readonly memslots, private or confidential memslots, or windows already in cooldown. Only windows crossing a programmable hit threshold are scheduled for proof. 4. Race-proof 2 MiB collapse proof. For one candidate window, KVM first takes memslots SRCU read protection and snapshots slots_gen0 from the active memslot set plus mmu_seq0 from READ_ONCE(kvm->mmu_invalidate_seq). If invalidate_in_progress is set, it aborts. KVM then takes the write side of kvm->mmu_lock and rechecks that the live memslot generation still equals slots_gen0, the invalidate flag is still clear, and the current mmu_invalidate_seq still equals mmu_seq0. With the lock held, KVM walks the TDP subtree for the 512 GFNs in the 2 MiB-aligned window and proves: every GFN resolves inside one writable shared memslot; no SPTE in the window is write-protected for dirty logging; no access-track or aging request is pending on any GFN; no memslot readonly or private bit is set; the effective R, W, X, and memory-type bits are identical across the window; and the queue generation plus metadata state still match the candidate snapshot. KVM then resolves the first GFN to host backing, takes a temporary reference on the head folio, and proves that the folio is compound or otherwise huge, that its mapped PFN range spans the full 2 MiB window, that the PFNs for all 512 GFNs are contiguous and derived from that same head folio, and that the folio alignment matches the 2 MiB window. If any GFN maps outside the same head folio, any PFN is missing, any permission diverges, any metadata page falls in the window, or mmu_invalidate_seq or memslot generation changes before install, the attempt aborts. Because promotion proof and leaf installation occur under the same write-side mmu_lock critical section, concurrent zap, split, dirty-log write-protect, or SPTE modification paths cannot invalidate the proof without also taking the same lock and causing the seq or state recheck to fail. 5. Install and flush strategy. Once proven, KVM replaces the parent non-leaf entry or equivalent 4 KiB subtree with one 2 MiB leaf SPTE referencing the same host physical base and permissions. The removed child page tables are linked onto a per-VM retired-page list. After dropping kvm->mmu_lock, KVM issues one single-context INVEPT or equivalent remote TLB flush for the VM, batched for up to 32 promotions or 50 microseconds, whichever occurs first. Only after flush completion are retired child page tables freed. This batching is correctness-safe because stale 4 KiB translations seen by running vCPUs map the same host PFNs with the same permissions until the flush makes the 2 MiB leaf visible; the batching only delays optimization visibility, not correctness. Any demotion or permission-tightening path uses immediate flush rather than the delayed batch. 6. Demotion triggers. KVM immediately splits the 2 MiB leaf back to 4 KiB or zaps it on any of: dirty logging enabled for the memslot; write-protect request for migration or logging; access-tracking or aging request over any constituent GFN; memslot readonly flip; memslot generation change; mmu_notifier invalidate_range_start or end affecting the host range; THP split, folio migration, reclaim unmap, or swap-out equivalent causing backing mismatch; newly observed indirect metadata page inside the window; queue UPDATE or UNREGISTER; nested virtualization enabled for the VM; confidential or private-memory mode active for the memslot; or a configured invalidation-rate threshold indicating unstable backing. 7. Optional debug-only telemetry embodiment. If explicitly enabled, KVM clones one EPT root per vCPU but duplicates only the 4 KiB leaves for registered control pages. No payload pages are duplicated, no huge leaves are duplicated, and actuation never depends on these roots. Default mode never clears A bits. An optional audit epoch may clear A bits no more than once per 10 ms per VM with one single-context INVEPT, and only when dirty logging is off, nested virtualization is off, confidential modes are off, invalidation rate is below a threshold, and telemetry is marked lab-only. Telemetry results are advisory and may be logged, but they cannot directly steer IRQ affinity, XPS, or promotion without independent non-telemetry policy approval. 8. Rollout. Phase 1 implements registration, metadata quarantine, and trace-only candidate scoring. Phase 2 enables canary promotion on explicitly tagged VMs and memslots. Phase 3 enables batched promotion by default for allowed hosts, with telemetry embodiment remaining off unless a debug knob is set.

2.6 Validation Plan

1. Kernel-correctness tests. Add KVM selftests that register vrings, stream virtio-net traffic, and concurrently toggle dirty logging, readonly memslots, access tracking, memslot replacement, THP split, folio migration, reclaim invalidation, and queue UPDATE or UNREGISTER. The required result is zero stale mappings, zero use-after-free of retired page tables, and deterministic fall back to 4 KiB leaves. 2. Proof-path assertions. Instrument the proof worker to log reject reasons for seq mismatch, folio mismatch, permission mismatch, and metadata conflict. Stress with forced mmu_notifier invalidations and ensure every seq or generation change aborts promotion before install. 3. Performance tests. Measure packets per second, vCPU cycles per packet, EPT walk cycles, iTLB and dTLB miss contribution, remote flush frequency, and host CPU overhead on 64-byte packet floods, mixed MTU workloads, and service-mesh style east-west traffic. Success threshold: at least 8 percent reduction in EPT-walk-related cycles on eligible workloads with less than 2 percent aggregate host CPU overhead from the worker and flush batching. 4. Flush-batching validation. Verify promotion batches do not exceed the configured 32-window or 50-microsecond limits, and verify demotion paths bypass batching and flush immediately. Confirm that child page tables are not freed until after flush completion. 5. Metadata-conflict validation. Generate descriptor chains that deliberately reuse payload pages as indirect tables after promotion. Confirm vhost reports the new metadata page, KVM immediately demotes or zaps the window, and re-promotion is blocked during cooldown. 6. Security and policy gating. Verify hard disablement when nested virtualization is active, when the memslot is private or confidential, when hardened policy forbids host access-pattern observation, or when dirty logging or access tracking is enabled. Confirm the optional telemetry embodiment is unavailable in those modes. 7. Telemetry containment tests. In lab-only telemetry mode, verify that only registered control pages are duplicated, that duplication stays within 8 leaves per queue and 256 leaves per VM, that duplicated leaves are skipped by KVM aging walkers, and that no production promotion path depends on A-bit resets or alternate roots.

4. Risks & Mitigations

  • Risk: promotion batches could add enough INVEPT overhead to erase TLB gains on unstable workloads. Mitigation: batch only same-permission promotions, cap batches at 32 windows or 50 microseconds, and auto-disable on high invalidation or demotion rate.
  • Risk: indirect descriptor table pages are guest-controlled and may alias with prior payload pages. Mitigation: quarantine every observed indirect page as metadata, demote immediately on new conflict, and require cooldown before any later re-promotion.
  • Risk: THP split, migration, reclaim unmap, or backing churn could invalidate the one-folio proof. Mitigation: proof under SRCU plus write-side kvm->mmu_lock with mmu_notifier-seq and memslot-generation rechecks, plus mandatory demotion on any later invalidation signal.
  • Risk: prior-art overlap with alternate-EPT or A-bit-sampling work. Mitigation: keep independent claims on single-active-EPT promotion only, confine alternate-root behavior to a debug-only dependent embodiment, and hard-cap duplicated leaves while excluding them from global aging semantics.
  • Risk: security or tenant-isolation concerns in confidential or hardened deployments. Mitigation: hard-disable the feature for nested virtualization, private or confidential memslots, and policies that restrict host observation of access patterns; promotion remains available only on shared, non-confidential memory.
  • Risk: implementation complexity in KVM and vhost lifecycle ordering. Mitigation: use explicit create, update, and destroy ABI transitions, stale-registration state, trace-only rollout first, and selftests that interleave registration changes with networking load and MM invalidation.
