1. Executive Summary

One-Liner

KVM introduces two MM-owned contracts for VMX/EPT on DRAM+CXL hosts: a dedicated, rate-limited tiering-hint path that exports only revalidated EPT accessed-bit heat, and a backing-provider leaf-contract query that returns `{run_len_pages, attr_id, epoch_cookie}` so 2M and 1G EPT leaves are published only when the provider proves a maximal PFN extent is still semantically uniform at publish time.
Novelty: The inventive step is not generic hugepage promotion or a routine notifier double-check; it is a provider-owned maximal leaf-valid extent query plus a provider-owned epoch_cookie that covers every correctness-critical at
Feasibility: The design stays inside mechanisms Linux and x86 VMX already expose: TDP-MMU walks, EPT accessed and dirty bits, memslot SRCU, `mmap_read_lock()`, transient no-fault page reference

2. Technical Invention Disclosure

2.1 Problem Statement

On x86 VMX hosts that combine local DRAM with slower CXL.mem capacity, Linux reclaim and tiering mostly observe host-side touches while a VM's true working set is often expressed in second-stage EPT state. Guest-hot pages can therefore look host-cold and be demoted to CXL, even though the VM is actively using them. In parallel, KVM large-leaf EPT promotion remains conservative because it has no authoritative MM contract proving that a leaf-aligned host-PFN run is still safe for one 2M or 1G mapping under concurrent migration, compaction, COW, shmem truncate or hole-punch, dirty logging state changes, private or shared transitions, or memtype and device-memory constraints. On DRAM-CXL systems those transitions happen often enough that false-cold demotion and missed huge-leaf opportunities materially raise latency and erode VM density.

2.2 Prior-Art Gap

Prior art covers EPT accessed and dirty inspection, KVM TDP-MMU aging, THP and hugetlb-backed promotion, `mmu_notifier`-driven zaps, and generic host reclaim mechanisms such as MGLRU. The missing pieces are specific and narrow. First, there is no policy-neutral, rate-limited, race-proof KVM-to-MM hint path that exports revalidated guest heat without directly invoking folio activation and destabilizing reclaim. Second, there is no provider-owned maximal leaf-valid extent query that returns both a completeness-checked attribute fingerprint and a provider epoch_cookie for the queried backing run. Existing KVM promotion logic uses local `can_hugepage`-style predicates and relies on later invalidation if a mapping becomes unsafe. That is materially different from an MM-authored proof that says: this exact run length is maximal, these exact correctness-critical properties are uniform, and any change that invalidates leaf eligibility must bump epoch before notifier completion. That provider-owned extent plus provider-owned epoch defines the novelty boundary and distinguishes the invention from known alignment checks, THP detection, and zap-after-the-fact behavior.

2.3 Proposed Invention

The invention adds two MM-owned interfaces. Interface A is `mm_tiering_hint_submit(mm, hints, nr_hints, heat_epoch, flags)`, which receives revalidated second-stage heat hints as tuples `{hva_start, nr_pages, granularity_order, access_class, weight}` and queues them into a bounded per-mm and per-memcg hint ring consumed by host tiering policy. KVM never calls `folio_mark_accessed()` or `folio_activate()` directly; the MM policy decides whether and how to convert a hint into age or tier preference. Interface B is `kvm_mm_leaf_contract_query(mm, hva_start, requested_leaf_order, out_contract)`, implemented by the backing provider selected from the current VMA or folio type. It returns `{run_len_pages, attr_id, epoch_cookie, max_leaf_order, deny_mask}`. `run_len_pages` is the provider-owned maximal contiguous PFN extent starting at `hva_start` that is valid for one EPT leaf of the requested order or smaller. `attr_id` is an authoritative provider-computed fingerprint over the full correctness-critical uniformity vector: backing kind, EPT memtype and PAT implications, shared versus private or encrypted state, writable-now status, COW stability, dirty-logging safety, NX hugepage eligibility, long-term pinning, migratability, devmap or pfnmap or ZONE_DEVICE exclusion, hwpoison or offline state, and truncate or hole-punch stability. `epoch_cookie` is a 64-bit monotonic epoch for the provider's leaf-eligibility domain. Any event that could invalidate a published large EPT leaf, including migration, COW break, truncate, hole-punch, shared-private conversion, dirty-log mode transition, pin-state change, or memtype change, must bump the epoch before `mmu_notifier_invalidate_range_end()` completes. KVM can publish a large EPT leaf only if the provider still returns a sufficient `run_len_pages`, the same `attr_id`, and the same `epoch_cookie` at publish time.

2.4 Architecture

Control flow:
1. Heat export path: the TDP-MMU aging walk samples only present leaf SPTEs with accessed equals 1 under `kvm->mmu_lock` and memslot SRCU, snapshots `{gfn, spte_pfn, leaf_order, mmu_seq0}`, and rate-limits each SPTE to one clear per `heat_epoch`.
2. Revalidation: KVM resolves `gfn -> hva`, takes `mmap_read_lock(mm)`, acquires a transient `FOLL_GET | FOLL_NOWAIT | FOLL_NOFAULT` reference on the current page, compares `page_to_pfn(page)` with the sampled `spte_pfn`, rejects devmap or pfnmap or missing pages, rechecks `mmu_notifier_seq`, and only then enqueues a hint. Any PFN mismatch, slot-generation change, COW break, migration, truncate, or notifier activity becomes a safe no-op.
3. Hint semantics: 4K SPTEs emit 4K hints; 2M and 1G SPTEs emit one coarse range hint tagged with `granularity_order` and weight 1, not 512 or 262144 synthetic 4K hits. When a 4K run is promoted to 2M or a 2M run is split, the replacement leaf or children inherit the current `heat_epoch_seen` marker so KVM does not double-count or lose signal within one sampling window. KVM owns A-bit clearing and clears a leaf only after a hint batch accepts the sample.
4. Huge-leaf admission path: KVM calls `kvm_mm_leaf_contract_query()` for the start HVA and requested leaf order, receives the maximal eligible run plus `{attr_id, epoch_cookie}`, builds the hidden large SPTE, then rereads `mmu_notifier_seq` and reissues the provider query before making the SPTE visible. Publish is allowed only if `run_len_pages` is still sufficient, `attr_id` is unchanged, `epoch_cookie` is unchanged, and the slot is not under dirty logging or NX hugepage split constraints.
5. Invalidation and teardown path: any provider epoch bump or notifier range overlapping a published huge leaf zaps that leaf to 4K and arms a per-range cooldown token so promotion retries are deferred until stability returns. Forward progress is preserved because the guest continues on 4K SPTEs and retries are throttled rather than spun in place.
Backing-provider matrix: anon private memory is allowed for 2M and optionally 1G when fully present, aligned, COW-stable, not under userfaultfd write-protect, and not in dirty-log mode; shmem or tmpfs and guest_memfd are allowed when inode indices are contiguous and not under truncate, hole-punch, page replacement, or private-shared transition; hugetlb may return native huge runs directly; generic file page cache is 4K-only in v1 unless the filesystem implements the contract; DAX, devmap, pfnmap, ZONE_DEVICE, poisoned memory, and offline memory return `max_leaf_order = 4K` via `deny_mask`.

2.5 Implementation Plan

1. Add `struct kvm_tiering_hint` and `mm_tiering_hint_submit()` in MM tiering code with bounded per-mm and per-memcg queues, token-bucket throttling, cgroup attribution, and drop-on-overflow semantics so guest heat remains advisory and policy-neutral. 2. Extend KVM TDP-MMU aging to sample EPT accessed bits at the currently installed leaf granularity only. KVM clears a leaf's accessed bit only after a hint batch accepts the sample, and at most once per configurable `heat_epoch` such as 10 to 50 ms. New 2M or 1G SPTEs start with the current `heat_epoch_seen` marker, and split children inherit the parent's marker, preventing double counting during 4K to 2M promotion or immediate split. 3. Implement the race-proof hotness revalidation algorithm: snapshot `mmu_seq0`; translate `gfn -> hva`; take `mmap_read_lock(mm)`; acquire a transient no-fault page reference; compare the current PFN to the sampled SPTE PFN; reject if the memslot generation changed, the notifier sequence changed, the page type is denied, or lookup would fault. Success enqueues a hint keyed by HVA and granularity; failure is a no-op with no retry in the hot path. 4. Add `struct kvm_leaf_contract` and `kvm_mm_leaf_contract_query()` plus provider ops for anon, shmem, hugetlb, and guest_memfd. The provider computes `attr_id` from the full uniformity vector and caps `run_len_pages` at the first PFN discontinuity, attribute mismatch, epoch-domain boundary, or policy denial. 5. Define authoritative epoch rules. Anon providers bump epoch on COW break, migration, mremap that changes the run, long-term pin transitions, userfaultfd write-protect, and poison or offline events. Shmem and guest_memfd bump on truncate, hole-punch, page replacement, migration, and private-shared conversion. Hugetlb bumps on split, migrate, remap, or attribute change. The new epoch must be visible before `mmu_notifier_invalidate_range_end()` returns. 6. Change huge-leaf publish to a two-read protocol: query contract, build SPTE, reread `mmu_notifier_seq`, requery contract, then publish only if both reads match and `max_leaf_order` still permits the leaf. On mismatch, KVM installs 4K SPTEs immediately. If a published leaf later loses eligibility, KVM zaps it once, repopulates 4K lazily, and sets `retry_after_epoch = current_epoch + cooldown`. Promotion is retried only after cooldown expiry and at least one stable contract reread, preventing livelock during repeated DRAM to CXL demotion and promotion churn. 7. Preserve dirty logging semantics by forcing `max_leaf_order = 4K` whenever a memslot is in dirty-log or write-protect logging mode, and by making `dirtylog_safe` and `nx_hugepage_ok` explicit elements of the provider attribute vector. KVM never publishes a writable 2M or 1G leaf when those bits are false. 8. Start with a strict allowlist. Unsupported backing types or ambiguous provider results return 4K-only. This keeps correctness ahead of coverage and creates a clean upgrade path as more backing providers implement the query.

2.6 Validation Plan

Correctness validation: stress concurrent page migration between DRAM and CXL, anon COW storms, shmem truncate and hole-punch, guest_memfd private-shared toggles, dirty logging enable and disable, userfaultfd write-protect, and hwpoison injection while running KVM selftests and targeted `mmu_notifier` race tests. Assert that any epoch mismatch, notifier sequence change, PFN mismatch, or provider denial results only in a dropped hint or a 4K mapping, never a stale large leaf. Performance validation: compare baseline KVM and the revised kernel on DRAM-only and DRAM+CXL hosts using OLTP databases, VDI, NFV packet processing, oversubscribed memory tests, and HPC workloads, including container baselines to isolate virtualization overhead changes. Measure guest memory latency, second-stage TLB miss rate, EPT walk cycles, VM exits, host DRAM residency of guest-hot pages, hint-drop rate, and promotion versus split churn. Stability validation: verify that per-memcg throttling bounds hint volume, that MGLRU generations do not oscillate under guest-driven noise, that dirty logging behavior is bit-for-bit unchanged when enabled, and that forward progress is maintained under synthetic CXL demotion and promotion churn where large-leaf promotion may be suppressed for extended periods.

4. Risks & Mitigations

  • Risk: guest-driven heat hints could distort reclaim or MGLRU behavior. Mitigation: use only `mm_tiering_hint_submit()`, enforce token-bucket throttling per mm and memcg, cap one accepted hint per folio or coarse leaf per heat window, and drop excess hints instead of activating folios in KVM.
  • Risk: repeated DRAM to CXL migration may constantly invalidate large-leaf eligibility. Mitigation: zap once, fall back to 4K immediately, and use epoch-keyed cooldown plus stability rereads before retrying promotion.
  • Risk: provider implementations may be incomplete or inconsistent across backing types. Mitigation: ship with a strict allowlist for anon, shmem, hugetlb, and guest_memfd; unsupported or ambiguous providers return 4K-only.
  • Risk: prior-art overlap with existing KVM hugepage promotion could be alleged. Mitigation: keep claims centered on provider-owned maximal extent, authoritative attr_id completeness, provider-owned epoch ordering, and the separate revalidated heat-hint channel.
  • Risk: dirty logging, write-protect logging, or NX hugepage constraints may be broken by large-leaf promotion. Mitigation: encode those conditions directly in the provider attribute vector and force `max_leaf_order = 4K` whenever they are active.
  • Risk: transient page lookup for revalidation could add overhead. Mitigation: use no-fault transient references only for sampled SPTEs, batch hint submission, and treat lookup failure as a silent drop rather than a blocking retry.
