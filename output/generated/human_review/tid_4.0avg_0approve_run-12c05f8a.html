2. Technical Invention Disclosure

2.1 Problem Statement

Background anonymous THP collapse and proactive compaction improve long-run locality and hugepage availability, but they do so by consuming CPU time, LLC capacity, DRAM bandwidth, and package energy at moments when the platform may already be thermally or power stressed. Linux today exposes indirect controls such as khugepaged scan rates and compaction_proactiveness, and x86 exposes package-energy counters through RAPL, yet there is no precise kernel mechanism that safely translates x86 package-energy behavior into admission decisions at the exact background mm work units that are optional. The core technical problem is therefore to use x86 RAPL microarchitectural energy information to modulate only optional background THP collapse and proactive compaction, without inserting MSR/powercap reads, sleeping behavior, lock inversions, or driver dependencies into mm daemons; without allowing stale package-to-node mappings after hotplug or NUMA reconfiguration; without biasing memcg fairness; and without ever suppressing mandatory allocator/reclaim work when fragmentation pressure or THP collapse debt reaches a threshold.

2.2 Prior-Art Gap

What prior art can do today: (a) khugepaged tunables and vm.compaction_proactiveness adjust daemon aggressiveness globally, but they are static or indirectly feedback-driven and are not conditioned on per-package energy epochs at the moment a specific background work unit is admitted; (b) generic token-bucket throttling patterns can meter arbitrary work, but they do not solve kernel-specific requirements such as wrap-safe RAPL acquisition, topology rebinding, seqcount publication, fail-open invariants, or bypass under allocator pressure; (c) powercap/intel_rapl and thermal frameworks expose platform energy/power information and can impose package limits, but they do not provide an mm-facing ABI that maps package telemetry onto NUMA-node-scoped admission of khugepaged and proactive compaction while preserving reclaim semantics. What prior art cannot do is the claimed combination: a dedicated sleepable provider contract for x86 RAPL package domains; deterministic package<->cpu<->node mapping and weighted node partitioning for multi-socket and SNC/CoD layouts; exact gating at khugepaged_do_scan() collapse-attempt admission and proactive_compact_node() slice boundaries; formal fail-open conditions for stale, reset, wrapped, trapped, constant, or ambiguous virtualized counters; and metric-driven floors tied to existing mm indicators such as fragmentation_score_node() and THP fallback/collapse-failure deltas. No known kernel mechanism offers those elements together.

2.3 Proposed Invention

The invention introduces an mm_energy_admit subsystem with three layers. Layer 1 is a provider ABI used only in sleepable context: struct mm_energy_provider contains a module owner, abi_version, refcount, and ops for get_topology() and read_package(); the provider is registered by an x86 RAPL shim that internally talks to intel_rapl/powercap or, where available, direct package-energy helpers, so MM never calls sysfs or driver code. Layer 2 is a delayed_work sampler that runs every epoch_ms, obtains a provider reference, reads MSR_RAPL_POWER_UNIT-derived package energy for each package domain, extends counters across wrap, detects resets or timestamp regressions, rejects constant/zero/trapped/virtualization-ambiguous streams, rebuilds topology when cpu/node generation changes, and publishes an immutable admission epoch. Layer 3 is a policy executor that converts each valid per-package delta_uj into a discrete admission tier using a hysteretic table: below low_energy_uj the package receives max_pkg_tokens; above high_energy_uj it receives min_pkg_tokens; between thresholds a bounded interpolation applies. Node budgets are then derived from package budgets using a deterministic node_share matrix. For a node wholly owned by one package, all node tokens map to that package. For SNC/CoD or node-spanning-package cases, node_share[node][pkg] is fixed for the topology generation as nr_online_cpus(cpumask_of_node(node) intersect package_cpumask[pkg]) divided by nr_online_cpus(cpumask_of_node(node)); node token reservations debit package buckets in descending-weight order with round-robin tie-breaking. Admission is checked only at two sites: (1) in khugepaged_do_scan(), after stop/freezer checks and after a PMD-sized candidate range is identified, immediately before the actual anonymous collapse helper is invoked, so scanning and mmap lock semantics stay intact; and (2) in kcompactd_do_work() only when the work item is proactive compaction, at each proactive_compact_node() slice boundary, never in direct compaction, allocation failure, or watermark recovery paths. Formal fail-open rules are explicit: allow unconditionally if no provider is registered, provider acquisition fails, snapshot age exceeds max_staleness_ms, snapshot flags report WRAP_RESEED, RESET, HOTPLUG_PENDING, TOPOLOGY_UNSTABLE, SUSPEND_RESUME, or VIRT_AMBIGUOUS, the node is below low watermark, direct reclaim/compaction is in progress, the kcompactd reason is not proactive, fragmentation_score_node(pgdat) exceeds frag_force_bypass, or the per-node THP debt computed from deltas of thp_fault_fallback plus thp_collapse_alloc_failed exceeds thp_debt_force_bypass. Minimum progress floors are not time based: if fragmentation_score_node(pgdat) is above frag_floor, one proactive slice per epoch is guaranteed for that node even when package energy is high; if THP debt is above thp_debt_floor, one collapse attempt per epoch is guaranteed for candidate PMD ranges on that node/package. The design expressly excludes kswapd reclaim, direct reclaim, direct compaction, and MGLRU aging from claims to avoid reclaim-policy overlap.

2.4 Architecture

Control flow:

RAPL package counters -> x86/provider shim -> mm_energy_admit delayed_work sampler
                    -> topology rebuilder + discontinuity filter
                    -> seqcount-published epoch {pkg_budget[], node_budget[], flags, gen}
                    -> khugepaged_do_scan() collapse admission
                    -> kcompactd_do_work()/proactive_compact_node() slice admission

Kernel-facing ABI:
- struct mm_energy_counter { u16 package_id; u64 raw_uj; u64 extended_uj; u64 delta_uj; u64 sample_ns; u32 flags; }
- struct mm_energy_topology { u32 generation; u16 nr_packages; cpumask_var_t package_cpus[]; nodemask_t package_nodes[]; }
- struct mm_energy_provider_ops { int (*get_topology)(struct mm_energy_provider *, struct mm_energy_topology *out); int (*read_package)(struct mm_energy_provider *, u16 package_id, struct mm_energy_counter *out); }
- struct mm_energy_provider { struct module *owner; refcount_t refs; u32 abi_version; const struct mm_energy_provider_ops *ops; }
- int mm_energy_provider_register(struct mm_energy_provider *p);
- void mm_energy_provider_unregister(struct mm_energy_provider *p);
- struct mm_energy_provider *mm_energy_provider_get(void);
- void mm_energy_provider_put(struct mm_energy_provider *p);

Lifetime and context rules:
- register/unregister occur in module init/exit or driver probe/remove.
- mm_energy_provider_get()/put() are RCU-protected and refcounted; readers in MM never call provider ops directly.
- get_topology() and read_package() are sleepable and may only be invoked by the delayed_work sampler.
- Unregister publishes PROVIDER_GONE and waits for outstanding refs before teardown.

Epoch publication model:
1. Sampler reads topology_generation and provider topology.
2. On any CPU hotplug, memory hotplug, cpuset rebuild affecting node CPU masks, or suspend/resume notification, sampler marks next epoch TOPOLOGY_UNSTABLE and fail-open.
3. First epoch after reset/wrap/resume is discard-only: baseline reseeded, no refill generated. Second valid epoch produces bounded refill, preventing token spikes.
4. begin_seqcount_write(); publish immutable package budgets, node budgets, floor bits, bypass bits, topology generation, and sample timestamp; end_seqcount_write().
5. Readers perform read_seqcount_begin()/retry(), then bounded atomic reservation against epoch-local counters.

Admission pseudocode for khugepaged:
allow = true
snap = read_epoch()
if snap.invalid_or_stale: return true
if node_thp_debt(node) >= thp_debt_force_bypass: return true
if node_thp_debt(node) >= thp_debt_floor: return reserve_floor_collapse(node, snap)
if !candidate_is_anon_pmd_collapse: return true
return reserve_pkg_and_node_budget(node, snap)

Admission pseudocode for proactive compaction:
if work_reason != PROACTIVE: return true
if node_below_low_watermark(pgdat): return true
if fragmentation_score_node(pgdat) >= frag_force_bypass: return true
snap = read_epoch()
if snap.invalid_or_stale: return true
if fragmentation_score_node(pgdat) >= frag_floor: return reserve_floor_slice(node, snap)
return reserve_pkg_and_node_budget(node, snap)

Concurrency model:
- seqcount protects readers from torn policy epochs.
- Per-epoch counters use bounded atomic reservation (CAS or atomic_inc_unless_ge style) so each admitted collapse attempt or proactive slice consumes at most one package slot and one node slot.
- If package reservation fails after node reservation, the node reservation is rolled back in the same epoch; on generation mismatch the helper fails open.
- Because readers never wait on sampler progress and sampler failure only sets bypass flags, there is no forward-progress dependency from allocator correctness to energy telemetry.

Fairness and scope invariants:
- memcg reclaim and memcg compaction fairness are unchanged because tokens are consumed only after the existing daemon has already chosen the node/candidate; there is no global workload scheduler.
- kswapd reclaim and MGLRU aging are out of scope and explicitly not gated.
- CPU/package/node associations are rebuilt on every topology generation change; stale mappings only cause fail-open, never mis-gating of a different node.

2.5 Implementation Plan

Phase 1: add mm_energy_admit core under mm/ plus include/linux/mm_energy_admit.h, guarded by CONFIG_MM_ENERGY_ADMIT and a static branch default-off. The core owns sysctl or debugless admin knobs for enable, epoch_ms, max_staleness_ms, low/high package-energy thresholds, frag_floor, frag_force_bypass, thp_debt_floor, and thp_debt_force_bypass. Phase 2: add the provider shim near intel_rapl so the shim registers mm_energy_provider_ops and hides powercap/MSR details behind the ABI; the shim exports package-energy counters, package IDs, and topology snapshots, and reports explicit error flags for wrap reseed, reset, suspend, trapped MSR, zero/constant streams, or virtualization ambiguity. Phase 3: implement sampler logic: package delta extension, discontinuity filtering, topology-generation rebuild, node_share matrix computation, hysteresis table application, and publication of immutable epochs. Phase 4: wire exact hook points. In mm/khugepaged.c, insert mm_energy_allow_thp_collapse(mm, addr, node_id, is_anon_candidate) only after khugepaged has identified a collapse candidate and after kthread_should_stop()/freezer logic, but before the actual collapse helper; do not gate the scan loop itself. In mm/compaction.c, insert mm_energy_allow_proactive_compaction(pgdat) only in the proactive branch of kcompactd_do_work() and at proactive_compact_node() slice boundaries; bypass all direct compaction and watermark-recovery callers. Phase 5: add hotplug and suspend integration using CPU hotplug state callbacks, memory-hotplug/NUMA rebuild notifiers, and PM suspend/resume hooks so epochs are invalidated and topology regenerated before gating resumes. Phase 6: add observability via tracepoints and vmstat-style counters only for post-fact inspection, not control: counts of allow, deny, fail-open, floor-bypass, topology-rebuild, and ambiguous-virtualization events. Phase 7: document invariants: direct reclaim unchanged, no MM-to-powercap lock coupling, no memcg policy change, and explicit exclusion of kswapd/MGLRU from claims. Example hook logic: if (!static_branch_likely(&mm_energy_key)) goto allow; if (mm_energy_allow_thp_collapse(...)) collapse; else skip only this collapse attempt and continue scan; for proactive compaction, if deny then stop after current slice and reschedule normal sleep, not busy-loop.

2.6 Validation Plan

Correctness validation has four layers. Layer A, API and discontinuity tests: use a mock provider to inject normal monotonic counters, wrap, reset, sample timestamp rollback, constant-zero streams, trapped-MS R emulation, provider unregister, suspend/resume, and hotplug churn; verify that invalid epochs fail open, first post-discontinuity epoch does not refill tokens, and no token spike occurs. Layer B, topology tests: emulate single-socket, dual-socket, SNC/CoD, and node-spanning-package layouts; verify deterministic node_share generation and rebuild on CPU or memory hotplug, and verify stale generations cause fail-open rather than wrong-node denial. Layer C, mm semantic tests: confirm that direct reclaim, direct compaction, watermark recovery, kswapd reclaim, and MGLRU aging never call admission helpers; confirm that khugepaged scan progress, stop conditions, and freezer handling are unchanged except that a collapse attempt may be skipped; confirm that proactive compaction only is gated and that fragmentation_score_node(pgdat) >= frag_force_bypass and THP debt >= thp_debt_force_bypass unconditionally allow progress. Layer D, performance experiments: run THP-heavy and fragmentation-heavy workloads on multi-socket hosts; measure pages_collapsed, thp_fault_fallback, thp_collapse_alloc_failed, fragmentation_score_node, proactive compaction activity, direct compaction frequency, allocstall, package-energy deltas, package temperature, throughput, and p95/p99 latency. Success criteria are: lower package-energy spikes from background mm activity, unchanged direct reclaim/compaction behavior, no increase in allocstall or watermark failures, bounded overhead from lockless snapshot reads, and demonstrable fail-open behavior under provider loss or virtualization ambiguity. Formal proof obligations to document are: (1) no forward-progress dependency because every telemetry fault becomes bypass; (2) every admitted unit is charged at most once per epoch; (3) no stale topology can deny work on the wrong node because generation mismatch triggers bypass; and (4) minimum progress is tied to existing mm pressure metrics, not wall-clock timers.

4. Risks & Mitigations

  • Risk: RAPL package counters may wrap, reset, pause across suspend, or appear constant under virtualization. Mitigation: mark such epochs invalid, reseed baselines, generate no refill on the first post-discontinuity epoch, and fail open until two coherent samples exist.
  • Risk: package-to-node mappings may become stale after CPU hotplug, memory hotplug, cpuset topology changes, or NUMA reconfiguration. Mitigation: maintain a topology generation number, rebuild package CPU masks and node-share matrices in worker context, and bypass admission on any generation mismatch.
  • Risk: a global budget could accidentally privilege one workload or memcg over another. Mitigation: no memcg token scheduler is introduced; admission occurs only after existing daemon logic has selected a node/candidate, so memcg reclaim and compaction fairness semantics remain unchanged.
  • Risk: gating the wrong mm path could alter allocator correctness. Mitigation: scope claims and code strictly to khugepaged anonymous collapse attempts and proactive kcompactd slices; direct reclaim, direct compaction, watermark recovery, kswapd reclaim, and MGLRU aging are unconditional bypass paths.
  • Risk: time-based throttling could suppress background maintenance long enough to worsen later direct compaction latency. Mitigation: use metric-driven floors and bypass thresholds based on fragmentation_score_node(pgdat), thp_fault_fallback, and thp_collapse_alloc_failed rather than timers alone.
  • Risk: MM-to-driver coupling could create lock inversion or sleeping-in-atomic bugs. Mitigation: all provider operations are sleepable and restricted to delayed_work; MM readers consume only seqcount-published cached epochs through lockless helpers.
  • Risk: excessive observability hooks could become a control dependency or hot-path burden. Mitigation: tracepoints and counters are post-fact instrumentation only, guarded and outside the decision critical section.
  • Risk: overlap with existing power limiting or thermal management may dilute novelty. Mitigation: claims are bounded to admission of optional mm work units using a dedicated provider ABI, topology partitioning, and explicit fail-open invariants rather than platform power capping.
