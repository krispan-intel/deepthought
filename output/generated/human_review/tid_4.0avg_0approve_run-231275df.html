2. Technical Invention Disclosure

2.1 Problem Statement

Linux reclaim, demotion, promotion, and large-page retention decisions are still driven primarily by allocator pressure, aging and refault heuristics, NUMA hinting faults, and generic access-bit evidence. On modern x86 servers those signals omit the most direct indicator of user-visible cost: where the CPU is actually paying memory-stall penalty, such as PEBS-observable load latency, offcore local-versus-remote DRAM response class, and DTLB walk pressure. The kernel therefore frequently reclaims or demotes the wrong folios, lets remote-access hotsets linger on the wrong node, and fails to preserve large executable or read-mostly mappings whose loss would amplify front-end stalls. A naive attempt to feed PMU samples into VM policy is unsafe. x86 PMU overflow arrives in NMI or hard-IRQ context, where the kernel cannot sleep, page-walk arbitrarily, fault in memory, pin user pages long-term, touch unbounded BPF state, or trigger reclaim or migration. Samples may refer to tasks that have exited, mm structs that have been replaced by exec, PIDs that have been reused, virtual addresses whose VMA has vanished, THPs that have split, or special mappings such as DAX, device-private memory, PFNMAP, VM_IO, or migration entries. Some useful counters, such as walk-duration signals, may not carry a trustworthy linear address at all. The missing piece is therefore not another heuristic but a bounded kernel contract that says exactly which x86 events qualify for per-folio attribution, how a sample is bound to a stable task and mm identity, how VA-to-folio resolution occurs without faults or long-term pins, how unsupported cases return explicit UNRESOLVED codes instead of hidden fallback behavior, how heat is stored without creating unbounded per-address state, and how any resulting reclaim or tiering action avoids deadlocks, reclaim recursion, tenant leakage, and policy-induced stalls. The problem becomes more acute on systems with tiered DRAM plus CXL memory, flash-backed page cache, and large executable mappings, because a bad reclaim decision now imposes not just refault cost but cross-node latency, demotion churn, or expensive flash refetch.

2.2 Prior-Art Gap

Existing Linux mechanisms each solve only part of the problem. Perf and PEBS can observe memory stalls but are fundamentally observational; they do not define a correctness contract for converting privileged samples into VM action. AutoNUMA can migrate pages, but it depends on hinting faults and does not consume PMU-derived remote-access evidence through a separate audited state machine. MGLRU and memcg-aware reclaim can bias eviction, but they do not accept a stable, lock-safe representation of x86 stall cost. khugepaged and hugetlb preserve or create large mappings, but they do not take x86 load-stall telemetry as an input while honoring VM locking rules. BPF policy plug-ins exist, but generic BPF attachment points do not define a safe control surface for reclaim and migration decisions. The prior-art weakness most likely to be raised is generic 'perf-guided hot-page' work. That overlap is narrower than the present disclosure because such systems typically rank pages from samples or traces without specifying: (1) an addressable-versus-aggregate telemetry split; (2) a stable task/mm identity contract resistant to pid reuse and exec; (3) a present-only, trylock-based VA-to-folio resolver with explicit unsupported-mapping matrix and no-fault guarantee; (4) a bounded fixed-cap heat store that does not allocate under pressure; (5) a reserve, refund, invalidate state machine for credits; and (6) a privileged eBPF policy capsule limited to aggregated histograms and bounded deltas only. The disclosure therefore occupies the gap between low-level observation and high-level VM action. It is not just 'use perf to find hot pages' and not just 'let BPF tune reclaim.' It is a concrete bridge from privileged x86 microarchitectural evidence to Linux reclaim, migration, flash-refetch guarding, and large-page retention under explicit failure semantics and lock-order constraints.

2.3 Proposed Invention

1. Sampling contract. The observe plane admits two telemetry classes. Class A addressable records come only from x86 PEBS or precise perf events that supply a stable linear address and data-source context, such as precise load-latency or offcore and mem-load-retired events configured with `PERF_SAMPLE_ADDR`, `PERF_SAMPLE_TID`, `PERF_SAMPLE_CPU`, `PERF_SAMPLE_TIME`, `PERF_SAMPLE_WEIGHT`, `PERF_SAMPLE_DATA_SRC`, `PERF_SAMPLE_IP`, and `PERF_SAMPLE_ID`. Class B aggregate records are non-addressable walk counters, such as DTLB walk-duration or walk-completed events that lack a trustworthy address; they may modulate node-level or page-class-level scoring but are never used for per-folio attribution. In the overflow path the broker does not walk page tables, allocate memory, touch arbitrary BPF maps, or trigger reclaim. It only copies a fixed record into a preallocated per-CPU ticket ring and increments a lost counter on overwrite. 2. Stable identity contract. Each task and each mm receives a 64-bit monotonically increasing cookie on fork, mm creation, or exec slow path. RCU lookup tables maintain cookie-to-object mappings until final teardown. The overflow record stores `task_cookie`, `mm_cookie`, and pid/tgid for diagnostics. Later, a translator worker resolves the cookies under RCU, takes `get_task_struct()` and `mmget_not_zero()` only if the cookies still map to live objects, and otherwise returns `UNRESOLVED_TASK_GONE`, `UNRESOLVED_MM_GONE`, or `UNRESOLVED_COOKIE_MISMATCH`. Pid reuse alone is never sufficient to bind a sample to an mm. 3. Non-faulting VA-to-folio resolver. The translator kthread uses `mmap_read_trylock(mm)`; lock failure yields `UNRESOLVED_LOCK_BUSY`. It calls `vma_lookup(mm, addr)` and rejects unsupported mappings with explicit reasons: `VM_IO`, `VM_PFNMAP`, `VM_MIXEDMAP`, `VM_HUGETLB`, DAX and devmap, device-private or device-exclusive entries, swap entries, migration entries, `pte_special`, `PROT_NONE`, zero-page aliases, KSM, and any case that would require fault-in or long-term pinning. Supported mappings are present anonymous folios, shmem/tmpfs folios, file-backed page-cache folios, and present PMD-mapped THP or other present large folios. For a supported leaf entry, the worker trylocks the relevant page-table lock, snapshots the present PTE, PMD, or PUD leaf, derives the folio, takes only a transient folio reference while the lock is held, rereads the leaf entry, and accepts the folio only if PFN and key flags are unchanged. Any mismatch yields `UNRESOLVED_RACE`. THP heat is folio-scoped with a 16-bin subpage offset histogram; if a split or collapse changes folio order before actuation, the entry is invalidated and rebuilt from future samples rather than silently smeared across new folios. 4. Bounded heat store. Resolved records update a preallocated per-node sharded hash table keyed by `{mm_cookie, memcg_id, addr>>PAGE_SHIFT, backing_class, folio_order_snapshot}`. Each entry stores a saturating stall score, remote/local ratio class, dirty and refault class, a 16-bin subpage histogram for large folios, page-size alignment bits, last-seen epoch, and actuation flags. The table has a hard cap per node and never allocates after initialization. When a shard is full, the lowest `score*recency` entry is evicted, or the new sample is dropped if weaker. Decay is deterministic, for example `score = (score*7)>>3` per epoch with expiry after a bounded number of silent epochs. Under VM pressure or worker backlog, the broker stops creating new entries, updates only existing hot entries, and clamps outgoing credits rather than competing with reclaim for memory. 5. Credit governor and actuation. At each epoch seal the broker converts heat into per-memcg, per-lruvec, per-page-class budgets: `protect_scan_delta`, `promote_budget`, `demote_budget`, `flash_refetch_guard`, and optional `page_size_guard`. Credits follow an explicit state machine `SEALED -> RESERVED -> CONSUMED or REFUNDED -> EXPIRED or INVALIDATED`. Reclaim credits are consumed only at scan-budget selection points such as `get_scan_count()` or equivalent pre-`lru_lock` planning code; they change anon versus file scan share or demotion allowance by a bounded delta and are never charged while holding `lruvec->lru_lock`, zone locks, or memcg charge locks. Promotion and demotion budgets are consumed only by broker migration kthreads that revalidate the folio and then invoke standard isolation plus `migrate_pages()`; the design intentionally does not consume credits inside the AutoNUMA fault path or in-vmscan demotion loops. On validation failure, topology generation change, memcg offlining, or policy version change, reserved credits are refunded or invalidated atomically. 6. Privileged eBPF policy capsule. Optional policy customization uses a privileged sleepable `mm_intent_policy` capsule attached at the broker's epoch-translation hook, never at NMI, IRQ, reclaim, or page-fault call sites. The capsule receives only aggregated bucket structs with no raw VA, PFN, inode, file name, or mm pointer exposure: memcg id, node id, backing class, page-size alignment class, latency histograms, remote/local buckets, refault density, dirty density, and flash-refetch class. It can emit only bounded signed deltas within verifier-known limits. The helper surface is restricted to constant lookup, time read, and `emit_delta()`, with no arbitrary map walking, no mm pointer dereference, no memory allocation, and no helper that can sleep unpredictably. The dispatcher enforces a maximum instruction budget, a maximum runtime per bucket, and a maximum number of buckets per epoch. Verifier failure, JIT failure, runtime timeout, or ROX publish failure causes deterministic fallback to the built-in C policy with zero net effect beyond counting dropped policy epochs. 7. Isolation and adversarial controls. Enabling the sampler requires `CAP_PERFMON` plus `CAP_SYS_ADMIN` in the init namespace; loading a policy capsule additionally requires `CAP_BPF` and a broker sysctl. Per-memcg quotas cap outstanding credits and per-epoch sample contribution. Policy-visible identifiers are memcg and node scoped, not task or address scoped. Executable large-page guarding is advisory only: on `X86_FEATURE_GBPAGES` systems it can preserve or nominate aligned ranges for existing hugepage paths, but the broker never installs huge mappings directly. Flash-backed file-cache folios may receive extra protection through `flash_refetch_guard`, but the policy cannot see block addresses or filenames.

2.4 Architecture

Data path and state machine:

x86 PEBS or precise perf overflow in `x86_pmu_handle_irq()`
-> broker overflow hook in `perf_event_overflow()`
-> NMI-safe per-CPU ticket ring writer: `ticket = atomic_fetch_inc(head)`; write fixed record; `smp_store_release(slot_ticket)`; overwrite increments `lost_nmi_records`
-> per-node `mm_intent_translate/<nid>` kthread drains local CPU rings by acquire-loading slot tickets
-> RCU cookie lookup for `task_cookie` and `mm_cookie`
-> `get_task_struct()` and `mmget_not_zero()` on live objects only
-> `mmap_read_trylock(mm)`
-> `vma_lookup()`
-> present-only PTE, PMD, or PUD walk plus page-table-lock recheck and transient folio reference
-> resolved folio update into per-node sharded heat table or explicit `UNRESOLVED_*` code
-> epoch seal into immutable `mm_intent_epoch` object
-> optional privileged `mm_intent_policy` capsule translates bucket histograms into bounded deltas
-> RCU publish of epoch object
-> consumer A: reclaim planner in pre-lock scan-budget selection reads `protect_scan_delta`, `flash_refetch_guard`, and `page_size_guard`
-> consumer B: per-node `mm_intent_migrate/<nid>` kthread atomically consumes `promote_budget` or `demote_budget`, revalidates folio, then calls standard isolation plus `migrate_pages()`
-> audit path compares post-action PEBS and aggregate histograms, refaults, and remote-access ratios
-> refund, invalidate, or decay into next epoch

Supported mapping matrix:
- Supported for attribution and credits: present anonymous private/shared folios, shmem/tmpfs folios, file-backed page-cache folios, PMD-mapped THP, and other present large folios.
- Supported but action-restricted: executable file-backed VMAs may receive protect or page-size-guard credits but not direct demotion.
- Unsupported and forced to explicit `UNRESOLVED`: swapped-out PTEs, migration entries, DAX/devmap, hugetlb mappings, `VM_IO`, `VM_PFNMAP`, `VM_MIXEDMAP`, device-private or exclusive entries, KSM, zero-page aliases, `pte_special`, and any mapping that would require a fault or long-term pin.

Data structures:
- Per-CPU ticket ring: fixed slots, no sleeping locks, no allocations, bounded overwrite semantics, nested-NMI-safe because writers reserve tickets atomically and readers validate per-slot ticket values.
- Per-node heat table: fixed-cap sharded hash keyed by mm cookie, memcg, page index, backing class, and folio order snapshot.
- Per-node epoch object: immutable arrays of credit budgets by memcg, lruvec, and page class plus generation numbers and quotas.
- Optional policy capsule dispatcher: stable C trampoline, RCU-published program pointer, ROX JIT pack, strict W^X.

Invariants:
- No page walk, no reclaim, no migration, no dynamic allocation, and no BPF map mutation in overflow context.
- No credit consumption while holding `lruvec->lru_lock`, zone locks, or memcg charge locks.
- Every unsupported or unstable sample terminates in an explicit unresolved code, never in a faulting fallback path.
- Every action is revalidated against current folio, memcg, node, and topology generation before consumption.

2.5 Implementation Plan

1. Add cookie infrastructure and ring records. Introduce monotonic `task_cookie` and `mm_cookie` allocation on fork and mm creation or exec slow path, maintain RCU lookup tables, and define the compact overflow record schema. Default record width is 48-64 bytes. 2. Implement the overflow hook. In the x86 PMU overflow path, admit only configured Class A addressable events and Class B aggregate events. Default sampling cap is approximately 2,000 addressable samples per socket per second plus 8,000 aggregate-only samples per socket per second, with local throttling when `lost_nmi_records` or worker backlog exceeds thresholds. 3. Implement the NMI-safe ticket rings. Each CPU gets a preallocated ring, for example 1,024 slots of 64 bytes each, or about 64 KiB per CPU. Writer protocol uses atomic ticket reservation and release-store publication. Reader protocol uses acquire-load validation; stale or overwritten tickets are counted as drops, never partially consumed. 4. Implement translator workers and the no-fault resolver. Run one `mm_intent_translate/<nid>` kthread per NUMA node. The worker resolves cookies under RCU, takes task and mm references, uses `mmap_read_trylock()`, performs present-only leaf walking plus page-table-lock revalidation, and emits either a resolved folio update or one of a closed set of unresolved codes. No GUP-fast fallback, no `FOLL_PIN`, and no fault-in path are permitted. 5. Implement the bounded heat store. Preallocate a per-node sharded hash, for example 64 shards and roughly 64K total entries per node at about 48 bytes each, yielding roughly 3 MiB of heat metadata per node. Use deterministic decay, low-score eviction, shard-local atomics, and a pressure mode that updates existing entries only. Key dimensions are memcg, page index, backing class, and folio-order snapshot. Backing classes include anonymous, shmem, generic page cache, executable page cache, and flash-backed page cache. 6. Implement epoch sealing and the built-in governor. Seal heat into immutable epoch objects at fixed cadence, for example every 50-200 ms depending on load. Publish budgets for `protect_scan_delta`, `promote_budget`, `demote_budget`, `flash_refetch_guard`, and optional `page_size_guard`, each tagged with epoch and topology generation. Apply deadband and hysteresis so small score changes do not churn budgets; for example require at least an 8/255 score delta or a 10% remote/local ratio shift before changing classes. 7. Integrate the consumers at lock-safe points. Reclaim integration occurs before `lru_lock` acquisition in scan-planning code such as `get_scan_count()` or an equivalent pre-lock planner, where credits may alter anon versus file scan proportions and demotion allowance by a bounded amount, e.g. no more than +/-12.5% from baseline. Tiering integration occurs in one `mm_intent_migrate/<nid>` kthread per node, which atomically reserves budget, revalidates the folio, then invokes standard isolation and `migrate_pages()`. Optional `page_size_guard` feeds only existing khugepaged or hugetlb candidate retention paths and never installs large mappings directly. 8. Add privileged eBPF policy capsules. Define a broker-owned `mm_intent_policy` program type or struct-ops-like attachment point executed only from epoch-translation kthreads. Inputs are aggregated bucket structs only. Outputs are bounded deltas only. Suggested verifier limits are a small fixed helper allowlist, bounded loops only, a per-bucket instruction budget on the order of a few thousand instructions, and a total per-epoch runtime cap on the order of one millisecond per node. Publish accepted programs through existing BPF JIT-pack and ROX machinery with RCU handoff. Any failure leaves the built-in policy active. 9. Expose observability and controls. Export unresolved-code counters, ring drops, epoch publish counts, quota hits, refund counts, and per-memcg credit totals through tracepoints and debugfs or sysfs, but never expose raw VAs or per-task addresses. Add sysctls for sampling caps, backlog thresholds, quota ceilings, and whether flash-refetch or page-size guarding is enabled. 10. Roll out in phases. Phase 1 records only and validates unresolved accounting. Phase 2 enables heat-store publication with zero-action shadow credits. Phase 3 enables reclaim bias only. Phase 4 enables broker migration workers. Phase 5 enables optional privileged policy capsules. Each phase has a hard disable path that returns the VM to baseline behavior without reboot.

Measure CPU cost of the overflow hook, ring writer, translator workers, and migration workers; target steady-state overhead below roughly one core per busy socket at default sampling caps. Measure ring-drop rate, backlog growth, heat-table occupancy, and policy runtime. Validate graceful throttling when workers fall behind. 4. Reclaim and tiering efficacy. Flash and page-size outcomes. On flash-backed page-cache workloads, measure major-fault count, read amplification, backend IOPS, and latency after enabling `flash_refetch_guard`. On large executable or read-mostly mappings, measure iTLB and dTLB walk counters, collapse or retention rate of large mappings, and code-page stall reduction when `page_size_guard` is enabled. Verify that policy-visible data contains no raw addresses, PFNs, or inode names; that only privileged users can enable the broker or load capsules; that per-memcg quotas cap amplification from a noisy tenant; and that verifier failure, JIT failure, runtime timeout, ROX publish failure, or topology change all force deterministic fallback to baseline behavior with remaining credits invalidated. 7. Robustness under low signal quality. Inject missing `PERF_SAMPLE_ADDR`, bogus data-source classifications, unsupported PMUs, and excessive unresolved rates. Confirm that Class B aggregate-only signals never trigger per-folio action, that low-confidence nodes revert to zero-effect mode, and that the broker can disable individual event classes or entire nodes without affecting baseline VM correctness.

4. Risks & Mitigations

  • Risk: PMU fidelity varies by x86 model and some useful counters do not provide stable addresses. Mitigation: split telemetry into addressable Class A and aggregate-only Class B, and disable per-folio attribution for any event class that cannot prove `PERF_SAMPLE_ADDR` quality.
  • Risk: resolver races on VMA teardown, THP split, or page-table changes cause false attribution. Mitigation: require cookie validation, `mmap_read_trylock()`, page-table-lock recheck, transient folio ref, and explicit `UNRESOLVED_RACE`; invalidate entries on folio-order mismatch rather than guessing.
  • Risk: metadata growth or worker backlog competes with reclaim and amplifies pressure. Mitigation: preallocate fixed-cap rings and heat shards, use deterministic decay and low-score eviction, stop admitting new entries under pressure, and clamp outgoing credits when backlog crosses a threshold.
  • Risk: lock-order inversion or reclaim recursion. Mitigation: consume reclaim credits only in pre-lock planning code, consume migration credits only in dedicated workers using existing migration primitives, and never execute BPF or PMU parsing while holding `lruvec->lru_lock`, zone locks, or memcg charge locks.
  • Risk: privileged eBPF policy causes unbounded work or stalls. Mitigation: restrict program inputs to aggregated histograms, restrict outputs to bounded deltas, forbid helpers that can sleep unpredictably, enforce verifier and runtime budgets, and fall back deterministically to the built-in policy on any fault path.
  • Risk: tenant information leakage or gaming. Mitigation: expose only memcg- and node-scoped aggregates to policy, require `CAP_PERFMON`, `CAP_SYS_ADMIN`, and `CAP_BPF` as appropriate, cap per-memcg sample contribution and outstanding credits, and avoid exposing raw addresses, PFNs, filenames, or per-task counters.
  • Risk: page-size guarding or flash-refetch protection interferes with baseline VM policy. Mitigation: make both features advisory and bounded, route large-page actions only through existing hugepage machinery, keep flash protection within memcg quotas, and invalidate credits on topology or policy-version changes.

⚠ Fatal Flaw: The draft lacks a kernel-correct MM locking/MMU-notifier model for VA->folio resolution and leaves core sections (Problem/Architecture/Implementation Plan/Claims) empty, so correctness and reviewability are not yet defensible.

  • The proposed VA->folio resolver path is not specified in a kernel-correct way: if you use get_user_pages_remote(FOLL_NOFAULT|FOLL_NOWAIT|FOLL_GET), you must prove it cannot fault, cannot trigger COW, and cannot block in ways that violate your stated ‘non-faulting’ contract; also define exact gup_flags (no FOLL_WRITE) and required lock context (mmap_lock rules) explicitly.
  • You claim ‘stable’ attribution via mm_cookie/mm_seq but do not define how those are read safely from the PMI/NMI overflow context without sleeping locks, refcounting, or races with exec/exit; you need a concrete, NMI-safe lifetime and publication scheme (RCU + seqcount/atomic generation) and explicit rules for PID reuse and mm teardown.
