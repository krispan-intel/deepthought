2. Technical Invention Disclosure

2.1 Problem Statement

Linux RX performance degrades when a subset of NAPI polls burns cycles on backend-memory stalls, LLC misses, NUMA-misaligned accesses, or ksoftirqd migration overhead, but the useful evidence exists at microarchitectural sample granularity while the corrective levers live in kernel networking code that is sensitive to fairness and lifetime rules. x86 PEBS can reveal where cycles were actually lost, yet the sample path arrives through perf overflow in PMI/NMI-like context where the handler cannot sleep, cannot allocate, cannot take RTNL, cannot assume RCU read-side state, cannot dereference `struct napi_struct` or `struct net_device` pointers, and cannot safely run policy code that changes queue behavior mid-poll. A practical invention must therefore solve, together and not piecemeal, the following: accurate attribution of a PEBS sample to the active RX execution region without pointer dereference; immunity to `napi_id` reuse by adding explicit lifetime generation; immunity to torn 64-bit reads on 32-bit kernels; exact publication and clear points for direct softirq and `ksoftirqd` execution, including no stale attribution after service-region exit; a bounded buffering path with deterministic drop behavior at high PMI rates; and a behavior-safe actuation rule that improves efficiency while preserving NAPI fairness, GRO invariants, driver semantics, and global `netdev_budget` behavior.

2.2 Prior-Art Gap

Existing facilities leave a specific and defensible gap. Generic perf/PEBS profiling can collect precise samples and export them to userspace, but it does not define a kernel-resident ABI by which a PMI handler can attribute a sample to an in-flight NAPI poll using a lifetime-safe key, a sequence-stable publication tuple, and no net-object dereference. `SO_INCOMING_NAPI_ID` and related `napi_id` plumbing expose a logical queue identity to sockets, but they do not encode lifetime generation, do not identify a particular poll invocation, do not define what is published when execution is in `net_rx_action()` but not inside a driver poll body, and do not specify memory-ordering rules that prevent a mixed `{ifindex, queue, generation}` tuple. RPS, XPS, RFS, and aRFS steer work based on flow hashing, socket affinity, or learned flow-to-CPU placement; they do not steer based on measured microarchitectural stall cost per poll, and they do not include any PMI-safe sample attribution contract. Static or manual budget tuning can change queue service aggressiveness, but it does not define a PEBS-derived, per-invocation `effective_budget` loaded once at poll entry with a hard floor, hysteresis, expiry, and proof that `napi->weight` and global budget knobs remain untouched. Comparison matrix in words: perf/PEBS gives precise samples but no NAPI attribution or actuation contract; `napi_id` gives queue identity but no lifetime, no poll epoch, and no PMI safety; RPS/XPS/RFS/aRFS give steering but rely on flow/socket affinity rather than where CPU time was actually lost; manual budget tuning changes service volume but is not driven by precise sample-derived cost vectors and does not define a safe commit boundary.

2.3 Proposed Invention

Introduce a three-interface kernel contract, with the independent invention centered on interfaces A and B and a dependent actuation embodiment in interface C. Interface A is a per-CPU publication ABI named `pebs_net_pub_v1`, cacheline-aligned and composed only of naturally aligned 32-bit-safe fields: `u32 seq`, `u16 scope_kind`, `u16 flags`, `u32 ifindex`, `u32 napi_id`, `u16 rxq_index`, `u16 txq_index`, `u32 gen_lo`, `u32 gen_hi`, `u32 poll_epoch`, `u32 policy_epoch`, `u32 budget_snapshot`, and `u32 reserved`. `scope_kind` is `0=NONE`, `1=RX_DISPATCH`, `2=NAPI_POLL`, and optional dependent embodiment `3=TX_COMPLETE`; the primary claims are expressly limited to RX and require `scope_kind=2` for queue attribution. Writer protocol: on `net_rx_action()` entry the CPU publishes `RX_DISPATCH` with queue fields zeroed; on `__napi_poll()` entry it writes `seq` odd with `WRITE_ONCE`, performs `smp_wmb()`, writes all payload fields with `WRITE_ONCE`, and completes with `smp_store_release(seq, even)`. On `__napi_poll()` exit it again makes `seq` odd, zeroes queue-identifying fields and sets `scope_kind=NONE`, then publishes the cleared record with `smp_store_release`; this explicit clear plus release ordering prevents attribution after service-region exit. `flags` includes at least `bit0=in_ksoftirqd`, allowing the same ABI to annotate whether the poll ran from direct softirq or `ksoftirqd` without changing queue identity semantics. Lifetime safety is provided by a 64-bit monotonically increasing generation split into `gen_lo` and `gen_hi`, allocated when a NAPI instance becomes live; the effective cookie is `(napi_id, generation64, poll_epoch)`, where `poll_epoch` increments once per `__napi_poll()` invocation. Interface B is a PEBS capture ABI: a perf event configured with `precise_ip` and sample contract requiring at least `IP`, `TID`, `CPU`, and `TIME`, with optional `WEIGHT`, invokes an overflow callback after x86 PEBS decode. The callback performs exactly one `smp_load_acquire(seq)` of the publication record, drops immediately if the value is odd, snapshots the remaining fields with `READ_ONCE`, re-reads `seq` once, and accepts the tuple only if the two `seq` values match and are even; no retry loop is allowed in PMI/NMI. Accepted samples are copied into a bounded per-CPU ring record containing the publication snapshot plus PEBS metadata, using only fixed-count 32-bit stores; if the publication is unstable or the ring is full, the callback increments a per-CPU drop counter and returns. Interface C is deferred actuation. A per-queue policy state keyed by `(ifindex, napi_id, generation64)` aggregates accepted samples by `poll_epoch`, computes normalized cost such as weighted PEBS cost per packet or per completed budget unit, and publishes `next_effective_budget` and `policy_epoch`. At the next `__napi_poll()` entry, the kernel snapshots `next_effective_budget` once, clamps it into `[budget_floor, incoming_budget]`, and passes that local value to the driver poll for that invocation only; `napi->weight`, `dev_weight`, and global `netdev_budget` are never modified. Optional slow-path steering uses the same policy state to request bounded RPS/XPS map changes, but those changes are explicitly outside PMI and occur only in process context under RTNL with RCU publication.

2.4 Architecture

The architecture has six concrete planes. Plane 1, region publication: hooks in `net_rx_action()` and `__napi_poll()` maintain the per-CPU `pebs_net_pub_v1` record. `RX_DISPATCH` is published around the dispatcher, and `NAPI_POLL` is published only around the actual driver poll body. Because the clear path sets `scope_kind=NONE` under the same even/odd `seq` discipline, samples taken after exit are either dropped or observed as untagged, never misattributed to the prior queue. Plane 2, lifetime management: each live NAPI instance receives a monotonic 64-bit generation at creation or registration time; the generation is stored in normal kernel context and only read as split `u32` halves by the PMI path. Plane 3, PEBS capture: a PEBS-capable event is opened per CPU; the overflow callback, reached after PEBS record decode in the x86 perf path, reads only the per-CPU publication ABI, local ring metadata, and perf-provided sample fields. It performs no allocations, no sleeps, no RTNL, no RCU-dependent dereference of net objects, and no retry loops. Plane 4, ring transport: each CPU owns a power-of-two ring with single local producer semantics and one deferred consumer; records are fixed size, head and tail are explicit, overflow increments `ring_lost`, and unstable publication increments `unstable_pub_drop`. Plane 5, aggregation and policy: a deferred consumer groups ring records by `(ifindex, napi_id, generation64, poll_epoch)` and forms cost vectors such as `stall_weight_per_pkt`, `miss_weight_per_pkt`, `samples_per_budget_unit`, and `ksoftirqd_fraction`. Policy is updated at most once every 8 polls or every 10 ms, whichever is slower, and only when at least a minimum evidence threshold is met, for example at least 16 valid PEBS samples and at least 64 units of work in the window. Plane 6, actuation: the mandatory action is `effective_budget` only, with `budget_floor = min(incoming_budget, 16)` as a hard floor and a default ladder of `{incoming_budget, 3/4, 1/2, 1/4}` not going below the floor. Reduction requires a high-threshold condition for multiple consecutive windows and `work_done` near the offered budget; relaxation requires a lower threshold for multiple windows or backlog drain; full rollback occurs on expiry, evidence loss, abnormal ring drop rate, queue teardown, or measured regression. Optional RPS/XPS changes are rate-limited to at most one map publication per queue per 100 ms, serialized under `rtnl_lock()`, and published with the existing RCU map-swap pattern so concurrent packet-path readers see either the old map or the new map but never a partially constructed one.

2.5 Implementation Plan

1. Add a new header and per-CPU state definitions for `pebs_net_pub_v1`, per-CPU ring metadata, and per-queue adaptive policy structures, with all publication fields limited to 32-bit-safe aligned stores. 2. Instrument `net_rx_action()` and `__napi_poll()` so the writer side publishes `RX_DISPATCH` and `NAPI_POLL` scopes, increments `poll_epoch` once per invocation, snapshots the current `policy_epoch`, and explicitly clears the record on exit using the odd/even `seq` protocol. 3. Introduce generation allocation on NAPI instance activation or registration and clear/retire generation state on teardown so a reused `napi_id` cannot alias a prior lifetime. 4. Register a PEBS-capable perf event per CPU with precise sampling and an overflow callback that requires at least `IP`, `TID`, `CPU`, and `TIME`, uses `smp_load_acquire` plus a second `READ_ONCE(seq)` for validation, and writes fixed-size ring records with no retry and no pointer dereference. 5. Add a deferred consumer in softirq-safe or kthread context to drain rings, merge records by `poll_epoch`, compute normalized cost vectors, and publish `next_effective_budget`, `policy_epoch`, expiry time, and hysteresis state into the per-queue policy object. 6. Modify the NAPI poll call site to snapshot `next_effective_budget` once at poll entry, clamp it to `[budget_floor, incoming_budget]`, and pass the local `effective_budget` through the invocation without modifying `napi->weight` or any global budget knob. 7. Implement the optional steering embodiment as a separate work item that takes a live `net_device` reference, acquires `rtnl_lock()`, revalidates `ifindex`, queue index, and device state, then invokes the same kernel helper path used by sysfs for RPS map replacement and `netif_set_xps_queue()` for XPS where applicable; map pointers remain RCU-protected, publication uses `rcu_assign_pointer`, reclamation uses the existing `kfree_rcu` pattern, and concurrent sysfs writes serialize under RTNL with last-writer-wins by `policy_epoch`. 8. Add tracepoints and stats counters for `unstable_pub_drop`, `ring_lost`, `policy_updates`, `budget_rollbacks`, `ksoftirqd_tagged_samples`, and map-publication outcomes so the safety contract is observable and auditable.

2.6 Validation Plan

Validation covers correctness, safety, and performance. Correctness: prove the publication ABI by litmus-style reasoning and runtime assertions that a PMI reader never accepts a mixed tuple; specifically test 32-bit kernels where `generation64` and timestamps are written as hi/lo halves, and confirm the single-pass `seq` check accepts only fully stable records or drops them. Service-region tests: exercise direct softirq, `ksoftirqd`, nested dispatcher windows, poll exit races, and device teardown; verify samples after poll exit become `NONE` or are dropped, never attributed to the stale cookie. Ring tests: saturate the PEBS rate and confirm bounded loss with no lockup, no allocation, and no retry path in overflow. Actuation-safety tests: confirm `napi->weight`, `dev_weight`, and global `netdev_budget` remain unchanged; verify `effective_budget` is loaded once per invocation and does not persist unless explicitly republished; and confirm the hard floor guarantees forward progress. Steering-safety tests: run queue teardown, unregister, and concurrent sysfs updates while optional RPS/XPS work items are active; verify RTNL serialization, RCU visibility, and rollback on stale `policy_epoch`. Performance tests: compare baseline versus invention on multi-queue NIC workloads covering tiny packets, mixed packet sizes, NUMA-misaligned memory, and induced LLC miss scenarios; collect throughput, p99 latency, `softnet_stat` time-squeeze, ksoftirqd residency, PEBS-weighted stall cost per packet, and CPU utilization. Novelty-support experiments: compare against plain perf collection, static budget tuning, and existing RPS/XPS/RFS settings to show that only the disclosed publication-plus-attribution contract can safely generate queue-specific, per-poll microarchitectural evidence inside the kernel and convert it into bounded actuation without violating networking invariants.

4. Risks & Mitigations

  • Risk: publication and clear points are too late or too early, causing stale attribution around poll boundaries. Mitigation: publish `RX_DISPATCH` around the dispatcher, publish `NAPI_POLL` only around the driver poll body, and perform an explicit cleared publish with release semantics on exit so post-exit samples cannot inherit the old cookie.
  • Risk: split 64-bit fields tear on 32-bit kernels. Mitigation: represent every 64-bit quantity as two aligned `u32` halves and accept a record only when the custom even/odd `seq` value matches before and after the read; otherwise drop without retry.
  • Risk: overflow handler complexity becomes unsafe in PMI/NMI context. Mitigation: restrict overflow logic to fixed-count `READ_ONCE`, `WRITE_ONCE`, simple arithmetic, and ring-slot writes; prohibit allocation, locks, pointer dereference of net objects, sleeping helpers, and retry loops; export counters for all drop reasons.
  • Risk: budget control could be viewed as fairness-disruptive. Mitigation: make `effective_budget` the only mandatory actuation, load it once per invocation, hold it constant for that invocation, clamp it to a hard floor, expire it automatically, and never modify `napi->weight`, `dev_weight`, or global `netdev_budget`.
  • Risk: RPS/XPS map changes could race with queue teardown, unregister, or concurrent sysfs writes. Mitigation: keep steering optional and slow-path only, hold a live device reference, serialize updates under RTNL, revalidate queue existence and device state before commit, publish maps through existing RCU patterns, and use `policy_epoch` to discard stale work items.
  • Risk: evidence quality may be too noisy for stable control. Mitigation: require minimum sample and work thresholds, use hysteresis with separate increase and decrease thresholds, bound update frequency, and trigger rollback when improvement is absent or regression signals exceed a configured limit.
  • Risk: reviewers may argue the disclosure is only an obvious combination of profiling plus steering. Mitigation: emphasize that the independent novelty lies in the exact publication ABI, single-pass PMI-safe attribution contract, and poll-boundary actuation semantics, all of which are missing from perf, `napi_id`, and RPS/XPS individually and in combination.
  • Risk: TX-side scope could dilute claim clarity. Mitigation: keep the independent claims limited to RX/NAPI polling and present any TX completion tagging only as an optional dependent embodiment with its own explicit scope field and no required queue-pointer dereference in overflow context.
