KEY PROBLEMS ADDRESSED:
1. Asymmetric LAPIC timer delivery latency on hybrid x86 (P-cores vs E-cores)
   - P-core delivery: 120-180ns (Golden Cove)
   - E-core delivery: 500-750ns (Gracemont) or 800-950ns with C1E exit
   - Divergence: 320-830ns systematic wakeup skew

2. Inefficient CFS load balancer group scanning
   - Current: O(n) linear scan through all groups
   - Impact on 288-CPU systems: 2-8us per load_balance() call
   - Gap: no awareness of timer delivery skew in scheduling decisions

PROPOSED SOLUTION COMPONENTS:
1. Topology-Clustered Calibration Engine
   - Derives hybrid_cluster_id from X86 CPUID (0x1A for Intel, Fn80000026 for AMD)
   - Per-cluster EMA-based timer advance calibration
   - Reduces calibration from O(N) to O(K) where K=core types (2-4)

2. Skew-Bounded CFS Group Pruning
   - Adds skew_bias_pct to sched_group_capacity
   - Enables early termination when no group can improve on current best
   - Target: 40-60% reduction in groups evaluated, 1.5-3.5us latency reduction

3. Runtime Recalibration & Thermal Adaptation
   - Lightweight recalibration every 10 seconds
   - Captures thermal throttling effects on E-cores
   - DVFS-aware (frequency changes automatically affect latency)
